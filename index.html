I am a Master’s student in Computer Science at the University of Maryland, Baltimore County (UMBC), with a bachelor’s degree in Computer Science. Currently, I am working as a research assistant at the Center for Accelerated Real Time Analytics under the supervision of Milton Halem. I am interested in machine learning, deep learning, physics-informed neural networks, transformers, and foundation models for scientific applications. My research explores AI-driven weather and climate forecasting, using deep learning methods for long-range prediction.

Research Interests

Master’s Thesis (in progress): Fine-tuning Aurora (1.3 billion parameters) on CERRA reanalysis at 5.5 km over Europe to increase spatial resolution, improve regional downscaling skill, and advance high-resolution regional AI-based forecasting. Aurora is Microsoft’s foundation model for the Earth system: pretrained on over one million hours of diverse geophysical data and designed to be adapted to multiple tasks (high-resolution weather, air quality, ocean waves) with modest fine-tuning cost; it delivers competitive accuracy at far lower computational expense than traditional NWP and supports specialized high-resolution forecasting workflows. My work adapts Aurora to CERRA’s 5.5 km grid, benchmarking regional skill, transferability across variables, and runtime/throughput gains relevant for operational downscaling. I design Observing System Simulation Experiments (OSSEs) for AI-based weather models to evaluate how new satellite observing systems and assimilation strategies affect forecast skill and long-range stability. For reproducibility, I maintain lightweight research codebases for these studies: Aurora OSSE (code) and FourCastNet-v2 OSSE (code).

More broadly, I work across AI/ML with interests that span foundation models, LLMs, multimodal/vision-language systems, and agentic workflows (tool-use, planning, and evaluation for autonomous agents). I’m interested in how representation learning, neural operators, and transformer-based modeling transfer across domains—from Earth-system forecasting to NLP tasks like retrieval-augmented generation, instruction following, and evaluation with multi-agent setups. I have completed graduate-level coursework in Machine Learning, Natural Language Processing, and Artificial Intelligence, and I enjoy building end-to-end experiments that combine scalable training, evaluation, and careful error analysis.

Previously, I worked extensively with state-of-the-art scientific ML models such as the Spherical Fourier Neural Operator (FourCastNet v2) and GraphCast, designing experiments that integrate data-assimilation ideas into inference to better align forecasts with observations and to study long-range behavior.
